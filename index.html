About

A concise academic profile

Welcome. I am Xiaojun Shang, an assistant professor in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Texas at Arlington.

My research interests lie in practical algorithms and systems for distributed computing, with an emphasis on computational efficiency, data-intensive applications, and emerging networked systems.

Before joining UTA, I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Engineering at Stony Brook University under the guidance of Prof. Yuanyuan Yang. I also received my M.S. in Electrical Engineering from Columbia University and my B.E. in Information and Communication Engineering from Zhejiang University.

Research

Core research directions

A focused set of topics at the intersection of systems, networks, and emerging computing platforms.

01

Connected and Autonomous Vehicles

Communication, sensing, and systems support for next-generation intelligent transportation applications.

02

Software Defined Networking

Optimization and deployment strategies for service function chains, flow routing, and resilient edge-cloud systems.

03

Machine Learning on Heterogeneous Systems

Resource-aware learning and scheduling across edge, cloud, and heterogeneous execution environments.

04

Distributed Quantum Computing

Network design, entanglement routing, and interconnection strategies for distributed quantum systems.
