Welcome. I am Xiaojun Shang, an assistant professor in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Texas at Arlington.

My work focuses on distributed systems, networking, and machine learning for computationally intensive applications, with current interests spanning connected vehicles, heterogeneous systems, and distributed quantum computing.

Before joining UTA, I received my Ph.D. in Computer Engineering from Stony Brook University, advised by Prof. Yuanyuan Yang. I also received my M.S. in Electrical Engineering from Columbia University and my B.E. in Information and Communication Engineering from Zhejiang University.

Research

Current directions

Connected and Autonomous Vehicles

Communication and systems support for intelligent transportation, cooperative perception, and infrastructure assisted applications.

Software Defined Networking

Algorithm design for service function chain deployment, flow routing, backup, migration, and edge cloud resource management.

Machine Learning on Heterogeneous Systems

Resource aware scheduling and system design for data intensive and heterogeneous computing environments.

Distributed Quantum Computing

Interconnection networks, topology design, and entanglement routing for large scale distributed quantum systems.
